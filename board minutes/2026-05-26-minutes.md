### Details

- Financial Updates and Contractor Hours: Elizabeth Barron reported that while the organization is currently overspending, they expect to be in the "green" in May and June. Contractor hours are being tracked to align with expectations and the overall management of hours is being monitored to keep individuals informed of their status. Nils Adermann noted that the tracking is based on flat rates that account for anticipated vacation time.
- Currency Conversion for Contractors: Nils Adermann and Elizabeth Barron addressed the difficulty of paying European contractors in Euros due to the Open Collective platform's single-currency limitation. Nils Adermann argued that the foundation should minimize conversion fees by encouraging more Euro-based sponsorships rather than requiring contractors to handle currency conversion. Elizabeth Barron agreed to coordinate with Open Collective to explore the feasibility and associated costs of this change.
- Alpha-Omega Grant: Elizabeth Barron confirmed the Alpha-Omega grant, thanking Nils Adermann for their assistance in the process. Tideways will invoice the foundation monthly for Volker's time, and Volker will track their hours as a foundation contractor. Despite one community member expressing concern regarding the use of AI, the overall response has been positive, with Volker already engaging with 25 projects in the first week.
- Contractor Updates and Ben Marks’s Contract: Elizabeth Barron reported that all contractors have received updated contracts, with three still pending return. Ben Marks’s contract is still delayed because no standard template currently exists, though work is ongoing to resolve this with Open Collective. Ben Marks continues to work with the Magento Association, which is viewed as a beneficial, overlapping opportunity.
- Sovereign Tech Fund Deadline: Elizabeth Barron informed the group that the deadline for the Sovereign Tech Fund project is September 30th. To ensure as much work is completed and invoiced as possible, the team is reallocating hours, with four additional people assisting Jakub. Nils Adermann clarified that since the contract is time-based, unused hours would result in lost funding; however, there is flexibility to move work to Composer/Packagist projects to avoid losing the money entirely.
- Infrastructure Migration: Joe Ferguson is providing support to Derick, which helps address concerns regarding the project's bus factor in this area. Discussions with potential sponsor for infrastructure will move forward.
- Impact Report and Strategy Document: Elizabeth Barron is finalizing the impact report and drafting a strategy document to outline plans for the remainder of the year . Nils Adermann expressed appreciation for Elizabeth Barron’s performance since joining the team.
- Open Source Conference Booth Proposal: Sebastian proposed that the foundation host a booth at an upcoming open-source conference (FrOSCon), noting that while organizing a full track is not feasible this year, obtaining a free booth is possible . Benjamin noted that the goal is to increase the foundation's visibility within existing open-source communities.
- Booth Volunteers and Material Approvals: The group discussed the need for volunteers to staff the booth, with suggestions to reach out to the PHP user groups. Nils Adermann and Sebastian agreed that Elizabeth Barron should handle the approval of a small budget for printing materials like flyers and rollups, making a formal board vote unnecessary.
- Future Conference Participation: The participants discussed potential future involvement in events, including a SymfonyCon in November in Warsaw. Nils Adermann requested that a volunteer lead the organization for future program tracks (FOSDEM), as the process requires significant effort.
- Event Calendar Management: Elizabeth Barron will manage the calendar for upcoming events, including PHPverse, UN Open Source Week, and Laracon.
- Print and Design Resources: Regarding the creation of promotional materials, Elizabeth Barron suggested delegating design tasks to Matt Stauffer. Nils Adermann suggested first asking Matt Stauffer if they have the capacity, and if not, exploring other internal resources.
- Employment Status with Open Collective: The group discussed Elizabeth Barron becoming a full-time Open Collective employee. Nils Adermann expressed a strong desire to retain Elizabeth Barron in a full-time capacity, regardless of the specific technical implementation.
- Employment Benefits and Procedures: Elizabeth Barron explained that Open Collective utilizes an external HR firm, which requires a pre-payment of one month's salary to cover potential severance obligations. This arrangement would provide benefits such as health insurance and a 401k plan.
- Bonus Payment Considerations: Nils Adermann raised questions about how performance-based bonuses would be handled within an employment contract. Elizabeth Barron noted that potential bonuses might need to be classified as cost-of-living adjustments to avoid higher tax rates, though the board will retain control over the specific amounts.
- Travel Reimbursements and Voting Procedures: Elizabeth Barron confirmed that travel reimbursement processes will remain standard as they are. Because there were only four members present, the group could not meet the 50% participation requirement for a formal board vote, so they decided to conduct an asynchronous vote on GitHub to approve the employment change.
